Some of my research interests

During my time as an undergraduate I researched with Robert Boyd's nonlinear optics group at the University of Rochester. I joined his group in February 2021 and began computationally exploring the effect of epsilon near zero (ENZ) materials on Dicke Superradiance. After making several advancements on the superradiance code I got distracted by a non-local aberration cancellation experiment. In this experiment, position-momentum entangled photons are created via SPDC. One photon encounters aberrations, and we correct for the aberrations non-locally by affecting the other photon. I wrote my senior thesis on this experiment.

In Fall 2022 I will begin researching with Dr. Andrew Weiner's group at Purdue Unviersity. I plan to research quantum integrated photonics including quantum frequency combs.
