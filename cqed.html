Some of my research interests

I am currently doing research with Robert Boyd's nonlinear optics group at the University of Rochester. I am computationally exploring the effect of epsilon near zero (ENZ) materials on Dicke Superradiance.

This research has taken me well beyond my undergraduate studies. I began by studying the Jaynes-Cummings model and optical Bloch equations. But this was not enough. I have since dug deeper, learning about the density operator master equation under the Born-Markov approximation and open quantum systems.

And yet I have only scratched the surface of CQED. The most interesting feature of superradiance to me is its application to ultra-narrow linewidth bad-cavity lasers. This is an interest that I plan on pursuing. My research has also peaked my interest in ultra-cold trapped ions/atoms, quantum information, quantum computing, and gauge theories in QED. I have a lot on my to-do list.

I will update this page and go much more in depth once I find an easy way to embed LaTeX in HTML. Also the CQED picture on my homepage was borrowed from Prof. Tilman Esslinger's ETH Zurich website.
