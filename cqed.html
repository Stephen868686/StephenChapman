Some of my research interests

During my time as an undergraduate I researched with Robert Boyd's nonlinear optics group at the University of Rochester. I joined his group in February 2021 and began computationally exploring the effect of epsilon near zero (ENZ) materials on Dicke Superradiance. After making several advancements on the superradiance code I got distracted by a non-local aberration cancellation experiment. In this experiment, position-momentum entangled photons are created via SPDC. One photon encounters aberrations, and we correct for the aberrations non-locally by affecting the other photon. I wrote my senior thesis on this experiment.

I have now completed my MSc research in Dr. Andrew Weiner's group at Purdue University. We are working towards building a QLAN with frequency bin entangled photons. A model for a quantum frequency processor (QFP) was proposed by Hsuan-Hao Lu and Joseph Lukens (1), both alumni of the group. Our vision is a QLAN with QFPs at each node. The main challenge is that the QFPs at different nodes must all be driven by the same 20GHz RF signal with sub ps stability. I achieved this with an RF over fiber technique to transport the GHz signal.

I am now starting my professional career at Penn State's Electro-Optic Center. The EO Center is a part of Penn State's Applied Research Laboratories that focuses on researching optics with DoD applications.
