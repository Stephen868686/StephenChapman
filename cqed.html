Some of my research interests

I am currently doing research with Robert Boyd's nonlinear optics group at the University of Rochester. I joined his group in February 2021 and began computationally exploring the effect of epsilon near zero (ENZ) materials on Dicke Superradiance. To work on the computer simulations I had to catch up on a lot of theory such as the Jaynes-Cummings model and the master equation applied to a two-level system.

After making several advancements on the superradiance code I got distracted by a non-local aberration cancellation experiment. In this experiment, position-momentum entangled photons are created via SPDC. One photon encounters aberrations and we correct for the aberrations non-locally by affecting the other photon. I am writing my senior thesis on this experiment, and I hope to publish a paper on it soon as well.

My time in Prof. Boyd's group has opened my eyes to areas of physics and optics far beyond my undergraduate education. Through this research I have become interested in many different topics including CQED, light matter interactions, quantum communication, and nonlinear optics.
